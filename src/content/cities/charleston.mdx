## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Charleston

- **Verify OT-specific credentials, not just IT security certs.** A CISSP is not a GICSP. For anything touching PLCs, HMIs, or SCADA historian servers, you want someone who holds a GICSP, CAP, or ISA/IEC 62443 certificate — or can show hands-on project history with industrial control systems. General IT security backgrounds don't translate cleanly to OT environments.

- **Ask for vertical-specific references.** Charleston's industrial base skews toward port logistics, chemical manufacturing along the I-26 corridor, and municipal water/wastewater. A consultant who's done five water utility projects understands SCADA historian replication and NERC CIP adjacency in ways a manufacturing specialist won't — and vice versa. Match the references to your sector.

- **Clarify whether they're doing assessment-only or implementation too.** Some firms deliver a vulnerability report and hand you the baton. Others stay through remediation and commissioning. Know which you're hiring before the SOW gets signed, because switching consultants mid-project after a network segmentation design is underway is expensive and disruptive.

- **Check their familiarity with South Carolina's regulatory environment.** If you're a water utility subject to AWIA 2018 or an energy facility with NERC CIP obligations, your consultant needs to speak that compliance language fluently — not learn it on your dime. South Carolina has active enforcement, and gap assessments that miss regional regulatory nuance are a liability, not a deliverable.

- **Ask one hard technical question before you hire.** Something like: "Walk me through how you'd segment an existing flat OT network without taking the process offline." Their answer will tell you immediately whether you're talking to someone who's actually done this or someone who read the Purdue model in a textbook.

> **Pro Tip:** Charleston-area projects involving port infrastructure or chemical facilities often have federal compliance overlays (MTSA, EPA RMP) that most SCADA consultants won't surface unless you ask directly. Bring it up in the first call.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Charleston typically run $10,000 on the low end for a focused cybersecurity gap assessment on a single-site installation, up to $150,000 or more for a full control system modernization — architecture design, PLC/HMI programming, network segmentation, and commissioning support included. Most mid-scope projects (assessment plus remediation roadmap for a mid-size water or manufacturing facility) land in the $40,000–$80,000 range with a 6–14 week timeline.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is scoping to assessment only, then discovering the remediation work costs three times as much and requires a different contractor who has to relearn your environment from scratch. If your system is more than 8 years old or has never had a formal OT security review, budget for both phases upfront and negotiate them together — you'll get better pricing and continuity.
