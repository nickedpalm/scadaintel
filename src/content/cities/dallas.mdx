Finding a qualified SCADA consultant in [Dallas](/locations/texas/dallas/) shouldn't feel like defusing a live PLC — but for most plant engineers and utilities ops managers, that's exactly what it is. The Dallas-[Fort Worth](/locations/texas/fort-worth/) Metroplex runs some of the most complex OT infrastructure in the country (ERCOT's main grid operations, Oncor's distribution network, dozens of midstream oil and gas facilities), yet there's no shortage of generalist IT shops happy to call themselves "ICS security experts" after a YouTube binge. This directory exists to cut through that noise.

## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Dallas

- **Verify OT-specific credentials, not IT certs.** A GICSP from GIAC, a CAP or CCST from ISA, or an ISA/IEC 62443 certificate tells you the person has passed a rigorous exam on industrial control systems specifically. A generic CISSP does not. Ask to see the cert and verify it directly with the issuing body.
- **Ask whether they've worked inside ERCOT's footprint.** Texas runs its own grid, and NERC CIP compliance on the ERCOT interconnect has quirks that catch out-of-state consultants off guard. If you're a regulated utility, make sure your consultant has real NERC CIP audit experience in Texas — not just familiarity with the standard.
- **Demand a network architecture deliverable, not just a report.** A solid engagement ends with documented Purdue model zone segmentation diagrams and a remediation roadmap tied to actual asset inventory. If they can't describe those deliverables in the proposal, keep looking.
- **Check references from similar industries.** A consultant who's deep in water/wastewater may be the wrong call for a midstream compression station. Dallas has concentrations of talent in both O&G and utilities — match the experience profile to your asset class.
- **Get clarity on subcontractor use.** Some boutique firms win the engagement and then staff it with junior engineers. Ask specifically who will be on-site for the architecture review and who will be writing the final assessment.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask for a single-page methodology summary before signing anything. Firms with real ICS depth will hand one over without hesitation. Firms without it will send you a generic IT security proposal with "OT" find-replaced in.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Dallas typically run $10,000 for a focused vulnerability assessment of a small facility to $150,000 or more for a full modernization project covering architecture redesign, PLC/HMI reprogramming, and network segmentation across multiple sites. Most assessments at a mid-sized facility — say, a water treatment plant or a regional substation — run four to eight weeks from kickoff to final report delivery.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is scoping by site count instead of asset complexity. A single facility with 600 field devices and legacy Modbus RTU across three networks is a harder job than three small substations with modern DNP3 and clean documentation. Push consultants to price against a preliminary asset list, not a headcount or site visit.
