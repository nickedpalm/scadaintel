Finding a qualified SCADA consultant in [Chicago](/locations/illinois/chicago/) isn't hard because there aren't any — it's hard because there are dozens of firms claiming OT expertise, and most of them learned industrial control systems from a PowerPoint. Chicago sits at the center of the Midwest's industrial backbone: water treatment facilities along Lake Michigan, natural gas distribution networks threading through six counties, and manufacturing plants in the collar suburbs that haven't touched their control architecture since the Clinton administration. The right consultant knows that difference. This directory exists so you don't spend three weeks chasing the wrong ones.

## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Chicago

- **Verify credentials before the first call.** GICSP (GIAC's industrial cybersecurity cert) and CAP (ISA Certified Automation Professional) are the floor, not a bonus. For anything touching the Chicago water system or ComEd's grid infrastructure, ask specifically about NERC CIP experience and ISA/IEC 62443 familiarity — those frameworks drive compliance timelines in Illinois utility contracts.

- **Ask for sector-specific references, not just industrial references.** A consultant who's done great work hardening oil & gas SCADA in Texas may be lost inside MWRD's wastewater control environment. Chicago's Metropolitan Water Reclamation District and Illinois American Water both have procurement histories — ask if they've worked anywhere comparable.

- **Get clarity on PLC/HMI vendor alignment.** The Chicago manufacturing corridor runs heavy on Allen-Bradley (Rockwell), Siemens S7, and GE iFIX. If your facility is Modbus-heavy or runs older Wonderware, ask your candidate to walk you through the last time they touched that stack. "We support all platforms" is a red flag.

- **Demand a written scope before any engagement starts.** Vague retainer agreements are how a $40,000 cybersecurity assessment turns into a $90,000 remediation contract you didn't see coming. Deliverables should be explicit: architecture diagrams, vulnerability assessment report, remediation roadmap — with page counts, not just category names.

- **Check for Illinois-registered PE licensure if your project has construction components.** Any consultant signing off on physical control system infrastructure changes at a regulated Illinois utility facility may need a licensed Professional Engineer on the team. It's not optional.

> **Pro Tip:** Chicago's industrial sector is heavily unionized, and some plant environments require consultants to coordinate directly with IBEW or UA reps before touching field instrumentation. Ask upfront — an experienced Chicago consultant will know this without being prompted.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Chicago typically run $10,000 to $150,000 depending on scope — a focused OT cybersecurity risk assessment for a single facility lands on the lower end, while a full control system modernization with PLC reprogramming, network segmentation, and hands-on commissioning support pushes toward six figures. Most diagnostic assessments take four to eight weeks; implementation projects run three to six months.

> **Reality Check:** The most common budget mistake is scoping for the assessment and not the remediation. Consultants who charge $25,000 to find your vulnerabilities will often charge another $60,000 to fix them. The better firms give you a realistic remediation estimate as part of the initial proposal — if they can't ballpark it, they haven't done enough of this work.

## Local Market Overview

Chicago's industrial control systems market is shaped by the presence of major utilities (ComEd, Peoples Gas, MWRD), a dense manufacturing base in the I-55 and I-90 corridors, and Illinois EPA regulations that put real compliance teeth behind water and wastewater SCADA requirements. The city's proximity to Argonne National Laboratory also means there's genuine deep OT security talent in the region — but that talent is concentrated, and the generalist IT security firms that market themselves as ICS-capable far outnumber the practitioners who actually are.
