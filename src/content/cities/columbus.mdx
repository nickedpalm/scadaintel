## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Columbus

- **Verify credentials before the first call.** GICSP and CAP certifications aren't marketing — they're proof someone has been tested on actual industrial control system security and automation design. Ohio's utility sector in particular demands this; AEP and Columbus Water Works both operate under strict OT security frameworks, and an uncredentialed consultant creates audit liability.
- **Ask for Ohio-specific references.** Columbus has a dense manufacturing corridor along I-70 and I-71, plus water/wastewater infrastructure that runs under OEPA oversight. A consultant who's worked in-state understands local regulatory nuance — not just the federal NERC CIP framework, but how Ohio utility commissions interpret it on the ground.
- **Scope the deliverable upfront.** Architecture diagram? Vulnerability assessment? Hands-on PLC/HMI programming? These are different engagements with different billing structures. Consultants who quote a flat "SCADA project" fee without scoping are either guessing or padding.
- **Confirm their OT/IT boundary experience.** Columbus's industrial base — Battelle, Honda of America's supply chain, the Rickenbacker logistics corridor — runs mixed environments where legacy PLCs sit next to cloud-connected SCADA nodes. Your consultant needs to understand network segmentation, not just the control layer.
- **Check insurance and E&O coverage.** For any engagement touching critical infrastructure, errors and omissions coverage isn't optional. Ask for the certificate before signing anything.

> **Pro Tip:** Ohio's manufacturing and utility sectors overlap more than most states. If your facility has both production automation and utility tie-ins (co-gen, water treatment on-site), prioritize consultants with ISA/IEC 62443 Cybersecurity Certificates — that standard covers the full OT security lifecycle, not just the network perimeter.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Columbus typically run $10,000–$150,000 depending on scope — a targeted cybersecurity risk assessment for a single facility lands toward the lower end, while a full system modernization with architecture redesign, PLC/HMI programming, and implementation support pushes toward the ceiling. Most consultants work in phases: discovery and assessment first (2–4 weeks), architecture and planning second, implementation third. Turnaround on a vulnerability assessment with remediation roadmap is typically 3–6 weeks from kickoff.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is comparing consultant day rates without accounting for deliverables. A $1,800/day consultant who delivers a 40-page architecture document and a working remediation roadmap is cheaper than a $1,200/day consultant who hands you a bullet-point summary and disappears. Get a fixed-fee proposal with defined deliverables — not a time-and-materials estimate that has no ceiling.
