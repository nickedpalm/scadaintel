## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Milwaukee

- **Verify OT-specific credentials, not just IT certs.** A CISSP or CISA is not the same as a GICSP or ISA/IEC 62443 certificate. The latter two require demonstrated hands-on knowledge of industrial protocols (Modbus, DNP3, EtherNet/IP) — the former don't. Milwaukee's water utility and manufacturing base runs on Rockwell and Siemens PLCs; make sure your consultant has touched that hardware.

- **Ask for Wisconsin-specific regulatory experience.** If you're a water or wastewater operator, your SCADA system touches PSC Wisconsin reporting requirements and potentially EPA AWIA 2018 risk assessment mandates. An out-of-state consultant who's only done NERC CIP work for Texas utilities will have a steep learning curve on your compliance stack.

- **Check their architecture deliverables, not just their pitch deck.** Ask to see a redacted network segmentation diagram or a sample vulnerability assessment report. Real OT consultants produce Purdue Model diagrams, network topology maps, and remediation roadmaps with specific CVE references — not 40-slide PowerPoints about "defense in depth."

- **Clarify the boundary between assessment and implementation.** Some firms only do risk assessments; others do end-to-end PLC/HMI programming and commissioning. Milwaukee's manufacturing corridor (everything from Komatsu to Harley-Davidson's legacy suppliers) typically needs both. Don't assume an assessor can turn a wrench.

- **Reference check within Milwaukee's industrial community.** The OT security world in Wisconsin is smaller than it looks. A consultant who's done solid work for We Energies or Milwaukee Metropolitan Sewerage District will have references you can actually call — and those clients will tell you the truth.

> **Pro Tip:** For any engagement touching NERC CIP-covered assets or water sector critical infrastructure, require that your consultant carry professional liability (E&O) insurance with a minimum $1M per-occurrence limit. A misconfigured firewall rule on an HMI network can shut down a plant. Make sure someone other than you is on the hook if it does.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Milwaukee typically run $10,000–$150,000 depending on scope — a focused OT cybersecurity assessment for a mid-size manufacturer might land in the $15,000–$40,000 range, while a full control system modernization project for a utility (architecture redesign, PLC migration, historian replacement, commissioning) can push $100,000 or more. Timeline follows scope: assessments wrap in 4–8 weeks; implementation projects run 3–12 months depending on plant complexity and change-window constraints.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is scoping for an assessment when you actually need an implementation. If your SCADA system is 15 years old and running Windows XP HMIs on a flat network, a $20,000 vulnerability report is going to hand you a $200,000 remediation list with no one to execute it. Budget for what comes after the findings, not just the findings.
