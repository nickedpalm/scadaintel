## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Memphis

- **Verify OT-specific credentials, not just IT security certs.** A GICSP or ISA/IEC 62443 certificate signals someone who understands the air-gap philosophy and the Purdue model — not just someone who pivoted from network security. CISM alone doesn't cut it for PLC/HMI work.
- **Ask for project references in your sector.** Memphis's industrial mix skews toward logistics, water/wastewater (MLGW serves 430,000+ accounts), and discrete manufacturing. A consultant who's hardened a water treatment SCADA system has a different mental model than one who's only touched oil & gas pipelines.
- **Demand a scoping document before any SOW.** Engagements here range from a focused NERC CIP compliance review to a full control system modernization with network segmentation design. If a consultant can't hand you a written scope with defined deliverables inside two weeks, that's signal.
- **Check their familiarity with Tennessee regulatory touchpoints.** TDEC (Tennessee Department of Environment and Conservation) has specific expectations for water/wastewater SCADA documentation. A consultant who's navigated that before saves you rework.
- **Ask who's actually doing the work.** Larger firms sometimes send a credentialed partner to close the deal, then hand the project to a junior engineer. Get the resume of the person on-site, not the one in the pitch deck.

> **Pro Tip:** MLGW and the Memphis area's industrial plants often require consultants to carry their own cyber liability insurance, not just general liability. Confirm coverage before you sign — finding out mid-engagement costs you time and leverage.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Memphis typically run $10,000 on the low end for a targeted vulnerability assessment or compliance gap analysis, and up to $150,000 or more for a full architecture redesign with implementation support and documentation handoff. Most mid-range projects — network segmentation, PLC upgrade support, or a NERC CIP readiness review — land between $25,000 and $60,000 with a 6–12 week timeline. Expect a discovery phase, a formal deliverables package (architecture diagrams, vulnerability report, remediation roadmap), and a closeout review.

> **Reality Check:** The single most common pricing mistake is scoping a project as "advisory only" to save money, then discovering mid-stream that the consultant's recommendations require hands-on implementation nobody else on staff can execute. Get implementation hours in the contract upfront — change orders on OT projects are expensive and slow.
