## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Stamford

- **Verify OT-specific credentials, not just IT security certs.** A GICSP or ISA/IEC 62443 Certificate means the consultant has been tested on industrial control system specifics — Modbus, DNP3, OPC-UA, PLC ladder logic. A generic CISSP does not. Connecticut's energy and water utilities have been under NERC CIP scrutiny for years; your consultant should be fluent in it.
- **Ask for references from facilities with similar process complexity.** A consultant who's done great work at a water treatment plant in Bridgewater may or may not translate to a natural gas compression station in Greenwich. The protocols overlap; the operational risk tolerance doesn't. Ask specifically: "Have you worked with [your DCS/PLC vendor] at a [your facility type]?"
- **Separate the architecture engagement from the implementation vendor.** The consultants who design your network segmentation should ideally be different from the integrators who install it, or at minimum disclose any reseller relationships upfront. Stamford has several regional integrators who also offer "consulting" — that's fine, but know what you're getting.
- **Confirm they understand Connecticut DEEP and EPA reporting requirements.** If you're in water/wastewater, your SCADA modernization touches regulatory reporting chains. A consultant who's worked in-state knows the DEEP's data reporting expectations; an out-of-state firm may scope that piece wrong and leave you holding the compliance gap.
- **Get a written scope with clear deliverables, not hourly estimates.** Architecture diagrams, vulnerability assessment reports, remediation roadmaps — these should be line items, not vague consulting hours. If the proposal is all T&M with no defined outputs, that's a negotiation problem before it's a project problem.

> **Pro Tip:** Stamford's proximity to [New York](/locations/new-york/new-york/) means many consultants price at Manhattan rates but operate here part-time. Ask where the lead engineer is actually based and how much of the engagement will be on-site versus remote — especially for commissioning and FAT work.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Stamford typically run $10,000 on the low end for a focused OT security assessment of a single system, up to $150,000 or more for a full control system modernization with architecture redesign, PLC/HMI reprogramming, network segmentation, and cybersecurity hardening across a multi-site operation. Scoping calls are usually free; proposals take one to two weeks; actual project timelines range from six weeks for an assessment to six-plus months for a full implementation.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is scoping a "cybersecurity assessment" when you actually need a full architecture review. Assessments find problems. Architecture reviews tell you how to fix them. They are not the same deliverable, and the price difference is significant — don't let a low assessment quote anchor your expectations for the remediation work that follows.
