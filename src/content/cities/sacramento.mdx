## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Sacramento

- **Verify OT-specific credentials, not just IT security certs.** A CISSP or CISM alone doesn't qualify someone to touch your DCS or PLC network. Look for GICSP (GIAC's industrial cybersecurity cert), CAP (ISA Certified Automation Professional), or ISA/IEC 62443 certificates — these are the credentials that indicate someone has actually worked in plant environments, not just read about them.
- **Ask whether they've worked with your specific control system vendors.** A consultant fluent in Rockwell/Allen-Bradley is not automatically fluent in Siemens SIMATIC or Schneider EcoStruxure. Sacramento's water utilities largely run GE/Emerson historian stacks; the food and ag sector skews Rockwell. Vendor-specific depth matters for both implementation and security work.
- **Confirm California PE licensure if the engagement involves design drawings.** For capital projects at SMUD, EBMUD's Sacramento service area, or any publicly regulated utility, stamped engineering documents require a licensed PE. Out-of-state consultants without California licensure create compliance risk downstream.
- **Get a scope that distinguishes architecture from implementation from audit.** Many engagements go sideways because the client wanted an independent OT security assessment and got a sales pitch for the consultant's preferred SIEM instead. Written scopes should specify deliverables: architecture diagram, vulnerability assessment, remediation roadmap — not "review and recommendations."
- **Check for NERC CIP experience if you operate bulk electric system assets.** SMUD and PG&E's Sacramento territory both have BES-connected assets. NERC CIP compliance work is highly specialized — most general SCADA consultants have never touched a CIP-002 asset categorization or written a FISMA-equivalent policy set.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask candidates for a sample redacted deliverable from a prior engagement. A consultant who can't show you what a finished vulnerability assessment or architecture review actually looks like — even anonymized — is telling you something.

## What to Expect

Engagements in Sacramento typically run $10,000 for a focused cybersecurity gap assessment on a small municipal water system up to $150,000+ for a full control system modernization at a utility or large industrial facility, including PLC/HMI reprogramming, network segmentation design, and implementation support. Most scoped projects run 6–16 weeks depending on site complexity and how complete your existing documentation is (it's rarely complete). Budget 2–4 weeks just for the discovery and as-built documentation phase if your system hasn't been formally documented before.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is scoping an "assessment" when you actually need implementation support. Assessments produce a report. Implementation means someone is hands-on with your hardware. These are different billable tracks, often by 3–5x. Clarify upfront which you're buying — a 40-page report with no remediation path is a sunk cost, not a solved problem.

## Local Market Overview

Sacramento's control systems market is shaped by two forces: California's aggressive water infrastructure investment (the Delta Conveyance Project alone will generate years of SCADA design and integration work) and the state's increasingly strict OT cybersecurity expectations following the Oldsmar water treatment incident and subsequent CISA guidance targeting small-to-mid water utilities. Consultants with water/wastewater SCADA experience and a working knowledge of California's Title 22 and AWIA 2018 risk assessment requirements are in particularly high demand here — and shorter supply than the general market suggests.
