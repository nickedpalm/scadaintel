Finding a qualified SCADA consultant in [New Haven](/locations/connecticut/new-haven/) shouldn't require a six-week procurement process and three rounds of vendor BS — but here we are. The local market is thin, the credential gap is real, and the consultants who know OT security from IT security are booked out or charging accordingly. This directory cuts through the noise.

## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in New Haven

- **Verify OT-specific credentials, not just cybersecurity certs.** A CISM from someone who's only worked in enterprise IT is not the same as a GICSP from someone who's debugged a Modbus loop at 2am. Connecticut's utility and manufacturing base — from UI/Avangrid's infrastructure to the water authorities running coastal treatment plants — requires consultants who've touched real iron.
- **Ask for references from comparable facilities.** New Haven sits at an interesting crossroads: Yale's campus utilities, Southern Connecticut Gas distribution lines, and a handful of legacy manufacturing operations in the surrounding corridor all have distinct SCADA profiles. A consultant who only knows power generation doesn't automatically translate to water/wastewater or process control.
- **Confirm NERC CIP familiarity if you're in the bulk electric system.** Connecticut is ISO-NE territory, and if your facility has any BES-adjacent assets, you need someone who can read a compliance gap analysis, not just write one.
- **Pin down deliverable format upfront.** Architecture diagrams, network segmentation plans, vulnerability assessments — these should be yours, in editable formats, not PDFs that evaporate when the engagement ends. Get it in the SOW.
- **Check for ISA/IEC 62443 fluency.** This is increasingly the baseline for industrial cybersecurity work in regulated environments. If they can't speak to the zone-and-conduit model, keep looking.

> **Pro Tip:** Connecticut DEEP and public utility regulatory filings are public record. If a consultant claims experience with a specific Connecticut utility or municipality, you can often cross-reference project timelines against rate cases and infrastructure filings. It's a five-minute check that eliminates a lot of resume inflation.

## What to Expect

Engagements typically run $10,000 on the low end for a focused OT security assessment at a single facility, up to $150,000 or more for full system modernization — architecture redesign, PLC/HMI reprogramming, network segmentation implementation, and post-project documentation. Timelines range from two weeks for a scoped audit to six-plus months for a full control system overhaul. Most consultants will want at least a site walk and a network topology review before committing to a fixed-price number.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is scoping a "cybersecurity assessment" without specifying whether it includes active testing, passive monitoring, or just document review. You can pay $25,000 for a vulnerability scan that tells you nothing actionable, or $25,000 for a hands-on segmentation review that actually reduces your attack surface. The price tag is identical. The deliverable is not. Define what "done" looks like before you sign anything.

## Local Market Overview

New Haven's industrial and utility footprint is denser than it looks from the outside — the Port of New Haven handles fuel distribution, Southern Connecticut Gas runs distribution infrastructure through the metro area, and the region's water authorities are in various stages of SCADA modernization under Connecticut DEEP consent requirements. That regulatory pressure is your leverage: consultants with specific experience in Connecticut-regulated environments understand the reporting requirements and won't be learning on your dime.
