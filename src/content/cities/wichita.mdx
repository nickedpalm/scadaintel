## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Wichita

- **Match credentials to your threat model.** A water treatment plant dealing with NERC CIP compliance needs different expertise than a natural gas processing facility running a Modbus network. GICSP covers the cybersecurity layer; CAP or PE (Electrical/Control Systems) signals hands-on engineering depth. Don't hire a pure IT security person for an OT project.
- **Ask for Kansas-specific project references.** Wichita's industrial base — aviation manufacturing, oil & gas midstream, municipal utilities — has distinct protocol stacks (DNP3, EtherNet/IP, Modbus RTU). A consultant who's worked on Evergy substations or a Koch pipeline facility understands the regional infrastructure in ways a remote generalist won't.
- **Demand architecture artifacts, not just reports.** Any serious engagement should produce network segmentation diagrams, zone/conduit models, and a remediation roadmap with prioritized CVEs. If a prospective consultant can't show you sanitized examples from prior work, keep looking.
- **Verify ISA/IEC 62443 familiarity before any cybersecurity scope.** The standard is the lingua franca of industrial cybersecurity — consultants who can't map your environment to SL targets and security zones are working from intuition, not methodology.
- **Check for conflict of interest on vendor recommendations.** Some consultants are effectively resellers for specific PLC or SIEM vendors. Ask directly whether they earn referral fees. Independent assessments are worth paying more for.

> **Pro Tip:** Kansas has a growing cluster of aerospace and defense OT security work tied to Spirit AeroSystems and McConnell AFB contractors. Consultants with ITAR-adjacent industrial experience often bring unusually rigorous documentation practices — worth seeking out even for non-defense projects.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Wichita typically run $10,000–$150,000 depending on scope: a focused vulnerability assessment with a remediation roadmap sits at the lower end; a full control system modernization with PLC reprogramming, network segmentation, and hands-on implementation support lands at the top. Most assessments take 4–12 weeks from kickoff to final deliverable.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is scoping only the assessment and treating implementation as "figure it out later." Remediation almost always costs 3–5x the assessment fee, and trying to hand off a vulnerability report to your internal IT team without OT expertise is how you end up with a patched firewall and an unprotected historian. Budget for both phases upfront or the report collects dust.
