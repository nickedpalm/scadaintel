## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Wilmington

- **Verify OT-specific credentials, not just IT security certs.** A CISSP means almost nothing for control system work. Look for GICSP (GIAC's industrial cybersecurity cert), CAP from ISA, or ISA/IEC 62443 certificates. These signal someone who's spent time in the plant, not just in a SOC.
- **Ask for references from utilities or process industries in the Mid-Atlantic.** Delaware sits under PJM's grid and feeds into the broader Chesapeake industrial ecosystem. A consultant who's worked with Artesian Water, Delaware City Refinery, or regional wastewater authorities understands the local regulatory environment and the specific equipment you're likely running.
- **Clarify the deliverable before you sign anything.** "Cybersecurity assessment" can mean a 10-page PDF or a 200-page remediation roadmap with Purdue Model diagrams, asset inventory, and a prioritized vulnerability list. Get specifics.
- **Check NERC CIP familiarity if you're in bulk electric.** Delaware's grid assets fall under NERC CIP compliance. A consultant who can't speak fluently about BES Cyber Systems and the CIP-013 supply chain standards will cost you more in rework than they save upfront.
- **Push on network segmentation experience.** The most common gap we see: consultants who design the OT/IT separation on paper but have never actually touched a Cisco IE switch or a Hirschmann in a live plant environment. Ask them to walk you through their last segmentation project, step by step.

> **Pro Tip:** Request a sample architecture diagram or redacted deliverable from a similar engagement. Any consultant worth hiring will have one ready. If they hesitate, that's your answer.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Wilmington typically run $10,000 for a focused vulnerability assessment on a single system up to $150,000 or more for a full OT security program — architecture design, implementation support, staff training, and compliance documentation included. Most initial assessments take two to four weeks; full modernization projects run three to six months depending on system complexity and site access constraints. Budget separately for hardware if the engagement includes network segmentation work — that cost often surprises first-timers.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake operators make is scoping only the assessment and not the remediation. A $15,000 gap analysis that surfaces 40 findings is only useful if you have a plan and budget to address them. Ask upfront whether the firm does implementation work or just hands you a report and walks away.

That mix of legacy DCS systems, older Modbus-based SCADA, and increasing pressure from CISA and state-level regulators to modernize means demand for qualified OT consultants is real and growing — but the local supply hasn't caught up yet.
