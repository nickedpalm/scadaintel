## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Nashville

- **Verify OT-specific credentials, not just IT security certifications.** A CISM or CISSP is table stakes for IT security — it doesn't mean the person has ever touched a PLC. Look for GICSP (GIAC's industrial-focused cert) or ISA's CAP/CCST designations. For cybersecurity work, ISA/IEC 62443 certification is the clearest signal someone understands purdue model segmentation rather than just firewall rules.
- **Ask for Tennessee-relevant references.** Nashville sits in TVA territory, and consultants who've worked with Nashville Metro Water Services, Tennessee-American Water, or the area's Tier 1 automotive plants (there are dozens within 60 miles) will hit the ground faster. General OT experience is necessary; regional familiarity is a multiplier.
- **Separate design engagements from implementation.** Some consultants do architecture and hand off to an integrator. Others provide end-to-end delivery including PLC/HMI programming and commissioning. Know which you need before the first call — scoping mismatches are the #1 cause of blown budgets.
- **Check their NERC CIP posture.** If you're connected to the bulk electric system or work with TVA-adjacent infrastructure, NERC CIP isn't optional. Ask candidates directly: "Have you managed a NERC CIP compliance review from evidence collection through audit?" A vague answer is a disqualifying answer.
- **Get a deliverables list in writing before signing.** "OT security assessment" means different things to different firms. Pin down whether you're getting an architecture diagram, a vulnerability prioritization matrix, a remediation roadmap with timelines, or just a findings report. The best consultants will already have a standard deliverables package — a sign they've done this before.

> **Pro Tip:** Nashville's construction boom means a lot of new facilities with greenfield control systems. If you're in that situation, ask candidates specifically about network segmentation and DMZ design from scratch — that's a different skill set than retrofitting security onto a legacy plant.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Nashville typically run $10,000 for a focused vulnerability assessment on a single system up to $150,000 or more for full-facility OT architecture design, implementation, and cybersecurity hardening across multiple process areas. Most mid-scope projects — a modernization design plus a risk assessment — land in the $35,000–$75,000 range with a 6–16 week timeline depending on site complexity and how much legacy documentation exists (spoiler: rarely enough).

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is treating SCADA consulting like IT project work and buying on hourly rate alone. A $185/hr consultant who scopes 600 hours is more expensive — and often slower — than a $220/hr specialist who's done your exact system type and scopes 280. Ask for a fixed-fee or not-to-exceed proposal. Anyone who refuses to offer one for well-defined scope is telling you something.
