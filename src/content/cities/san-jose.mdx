The same ZIP codes that house Cisco's industrial security division and half the Bay Area's OT talent pool also harbor a flood of IT generalists who've rebranded themselves as "industrial cybersecurity experts" after one Coursera certificate. This directory exists to help plant engineers and utility operators at organizations like the Santa Clara Valley Water District or PG&E's transmission substations find the real ones, fast.

## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in San Jose

- **Require OT-specific credentials, not just IT certifications.** A GICSP (GIAC's industrial cyber cert) or ISA CAP tells you someone has actually worked with PLCs, HMIs, and SCADA historian systems — not just firewalls and endpoints. A CISSP alone is not enough for an OT engagement.
- **Ask specifically about your control system vendor.** There's a meaningful difference between someone who's done Rockwell ControlLogix deployments for water treatment and someone who's "familiar with industrial systems generally." San Jose's water and wastewater infrastructure runs a mix of Wonderware, GE iFIX, and Allen-Bradley platforms — make sure the resume matches.
- **Verify NERC CIP experience if you're in the electric sector.** PG&E territory means CIP-002 through CIP-013 compliance is a real obligation. Ask for a work sample or reference from a prior NERC CIP gap assessment, not just a line item on a resume.
- **Separate the architecture work from the hands-on implementation.** Some of the best SCADA consultants in the Bay Area are pure architects — they'll design your network segmentation and OT DMZ all day, but they won't rack the Purdue model switches themselves. Know which deliverable you're actually buying.
- **Check for conflict of interest with vendors.** Several Bay Area consultants are on retainer with specific DCS or SCADA vendors. That's not always a dealbreaker, but if they recommend a platform before your discovery call is over, ask why.

> **Pro Tip:** California's water agencies face AB 2344 and additional state-level reporting obligations on top of federal AWIA 2018 requirements. Ask any candidate whether they've worked with a California water utility before — the compliance layer is specific enough to matter.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in San Jose typically run $10,000 for a focused vulnerability assessment on a single system up to $150,000 or more for a full OT network redesign and implementation across a multi-site facility. Initial scoping calls are usually free; a proper discovery and site walk-through adds 1–2 weeks before a formal proposal. Full architecture-through-implementation projects run 3–6 months depending on system complexity.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake operators make is scoping an "IT security audit" and getting an IT security audit. A consultant who prices your engagement at $8,000 for a "SCADA pen test" and delivers a Nessus scan with no OT context has cost you more than the bill — they've given you a false sense of security. Industrial control system assessments require passive network monitoring, asset discovery with OT-aware tools like Claroty or Dragos, and an engineer who understands process impact, not just CVE scores.
