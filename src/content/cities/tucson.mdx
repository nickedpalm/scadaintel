## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Tucson

- **Verify OT-specific credentials, not just IT certs.** A general cybersecurity background doesn't cut it for industrial environments. Look for GICSP (GIAC's gold standard for ICS security), CAP (ISA's automation professional cert), or documented ISA/IEC 62443 coursework. A PE license in electrical or control systems is a strong signal for infrastructure-heavy engagements.
- **Ask for Arizona utility or industrial references specifically.** Tucson Water, TEP, and the region's mining and defense manufacturing base have distinct regulatory environments. A consultant who's worked with Arizona utilities or Pima County infrastructure projects will hit the ground running on permitting, ADEQ interface points, and local NERC CIP interpretations.
- **Scope before you sign.** SCADA engagements balloon in scope faster than almost any other technical project. Get a written deliverable list — architecture diagrams, vulnerability assessment report, remediation roadmap — before work starts. Vague SOWs are how $30K projects become $90K projects.
- **Probe their PLC/HMI bench experience.** Architecture diagrams are easy. Ask whether they've done hands-on Allen-Bradley, Siemens, or Inductive Automation Ignition work in the field. The best consultants in Tucson's industrial and water/wastewater sector have grease under their fingernails, not just Visio files.
- **Check for conflict of interest with vendors.** Some consultants are effectively sales engineers for specific hardware vendors. That's not disqualifying, but you should know going in whose ecosystem they're likely to recommend.

> **Pro Tip:** For NERC CIP or DOD-adjacent work near Davis-Monthan AFB's supply chain, ask explicitly whether the consultant has held a security clearance or worked within CMMC/DFARS-regulated environments. It narrows the field fast but filters for exactly the right people.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Tucson typically run $10,000–$150,000 depending on scope — a focused OT cybersecurity risk assessment for a water treatment facility sits at the lower end, while a full control system modernization for a mining or utility operation with PLC replacement, network segmentation, and NERC CIP documentation lands at the top. Most projects run 6–16 weeks from kickoff to final deliverable, with a site assessment in weeks one and two.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is budgeting for the assessment and not the remediation. Consultants will hand you a vulnerability report with 40 findings. Implementing those findings — patching, network redesign, vendor coordination — is frequently 3–5x the cost of the assessment itself. Build that into your capital planning from day one.
