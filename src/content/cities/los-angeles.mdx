The LA basin runs some of the most complex industrial infrastructure in the country — LADWP's power grid, the Port of LA's terminal automation, MWD's water treatment network — and the consultants those operations trust aren't easy to find through a Google search. This directory exists to cut through the noise.

## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Los Angeles

- **Verify OT-specific credentials, not just IT certs.** A CISSP means nothing if the person has never done network segmentation between a historian and a DCS. Look for GICSP, CAP, or ISA/IEC 62443 certificates — these signal someone who understands the operational constraints of industrial environments, not just enterprise IT.

- **Ask for California-relevant references.** LA's utility sector operates under CPUC oversight, and water agencies answer to State Water Board regulations on top of federal requirements. A consultant who's worked with LADWP, LACSD, or a Southern California municipal utility understands the compliance layer. Ask specifically.

- **Check their NERC CIP exposure if you're in power.** The Western Interconnection has some of the tightest NERC CIP enforcement in the country. If your project touches bulk electric system assets, you need someone who's done CIP-002 through CIP-013 assessments — not someone who's read the standards.

- **Scope the engagement in writing before anyone touches a keyboard.** The difference between a $12,000 vulnerability assessment and a $90,000 remediation project often comes down to scope creep. Get a statement of work that defines deliverables — architecture diagrams, risk register, remediation roadmap — before work begins.

- **Demand air-gap experience if it applies.** Many LA-area manufacturing and defense-adjacent facilities maintain air-gapped or unidirectional architectures. Not every consultant has worked in those environments. Ask directly: "Have you designed data flows for a one-way gateway?" If they hesitate, that's your answer.

> **Pro Tip:** The LA basin has a dense cluster of EPC firms that do SCADA as a side service to their primary controls work. That's fine for greenfield installations, but if you're doing a cybersecurity assessment or NERC CIP audit, you want an independent consultant with no skin in the game on the implementation side. Conflicts of interest in OT security assessments are more common than the industry admits.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Los Angeles typically run $10,000–$150,000 depending on scope — a focused OT vulnerability assessment for a single facility sits in the $15,000–$35,000 range, while a full architecture redesign with implementation support for a multi-site utility can push well past six figures. Most projects have a discovery-to-deliverable cycle of 6–12 weeks; emergency incident response engagements compress that significantly.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake plant engineers make is scoping for the assessment and forgetting the remediation. A $20,000 vulnerability assessment that surfaces 40 critical findings doesn't fix anything on its own — budget for both phases, or at minimum confirm the consultant can support remediation before you sign.
