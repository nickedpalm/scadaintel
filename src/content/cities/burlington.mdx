## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Burlington

- **Verify certifications before the first call.** GICSP from GIAC, CAP from ISA, and the ISA/IEC 62443 certificate are the three that signal someone has put in real hours on OT security and automation — not just IT professionals who've renamed themselves. PE licensure matters if your project touches anything regulated by Vermont's Public Utility Commission.
- **Ask specifically about your sector.** Burlington's SCADA needs skew toward water/wastewater (Champlain Water District serves the region), municipal electric (Burlington Electric Department runs its own distribution grid), and light manufacturing. A consultant who's spent their career on oil pipelines isn't automatically qualified for a drinking water SCADA overhaul.
- **Get a sample deliverable.** Any consultant worth hiring can show you a redacted architecture diagram or a sanitized vulnerability assessment. If they hedge on this, walk.
- **Check NERC CIP familiarity if you're in electric utilities.** Green Mountain Power and BED both operate under NERC CIP obligations. Consultants who've navigated CIP-002 through CIP-013 compliance reviews are a genuinely different skill set than those who haven't.
- **Run a reference check on a completed project, not a reference call.** Ask to speak with an operations engineer at a past client — not a project manager. You want to know if the system they designed is still running reliably 18 months later.

> **Pro Tip:** Vermont has a handful of regional engineering firms that do SCADA work as a side service alongside civil or electrical work. For a serious modernization engagement or a cybersecurity audit, you want a specialist whose primary practice is control systems — not someone who tabs over to a PLC ladder logic editor once a quarter.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Vermont typically run $10,000 on the low end for a focused vulnerability assessment or network segmentation review, up to $150,000 or more for a full control system modernization — architecture design, PLC/HMI programming, cybersecurity hardening, and implementation support included. Most projects land somewhere in the $30,000–$80,000 range. Timeline from kickoff to final deliverable is typically 6–16 weeks depending on scope.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is scoping an "assessment only" engagement when you actually need implementation support, then treating the resulting remediation roadmap as a second separate project. The consultant who wrote your vulnerability assessment is the one who knows where all the bodies are buried — budget for continuity upfront, or you'll pay a premium for a new consultant to come up to speed on findings they didn't generate.

## Local Market Overview

Burlington sits at the operational center of Vermont's utility and water infrastructure, with Burlington Electric Department running one of the few municipally-owned distribution systems in New England and Champlain Water District managing treatment and distribution for roughly 70,000 people across Chittenden County — both environments where aging control systems and OT security gaps are live concerns, not theoretical ones. The consulting talent pool here is thin by design; most OT specialists who work in Vermont are either based in Burlington or travel in from Burlington for regional projects, which means scheduling lead times can run 4–8 weeks even for discovery calls.
