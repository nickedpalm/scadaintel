## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Jacksonville

- **Verify OT-specific credentials, not just IT security certs.** A GICSP (GIAC Global Industrial Cyber Security Professional) or ISA CAP tells you someone has been tested on control system architecture, not just enterprise networking. CISM alone doesn't cut it for PLC/HMI work.
- **Ask about experience with your specific protocol stack.** DNP3, Modbus, and EtherNet/IP are not interchangeable. A consultant who's spent their career on water/wastewater SCADA may not have depth in the substation automation protocols JEA-adjacent projects require.
- **Check Florida PE licensure if the engagement involves control system design that touches safety systems.** For projects where the deliverable includes signed architecture drawings or formal engineering assessments, Florida Board of Professional Engineers licensure matters.
- **Push for an ISA/IEC 62443 methodology.** It's the industry standard for OT cybersecurity risk assessments. If a consultant can't map their process to 62443 zones and conduits, they're winging it.
- **Get references from at least one utility or critical infrastructure client in the Southeast.** Florida's regulatory environment (FDEP for water, FERC/NERC CIP for energy) has specific compliance wrinkles. You want someone who's navigated those before, not learned on your dime.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask any candidate what their process is for handling air-gapped networks. The answer tells you immediately whether they've done real OT work or just read the whitepapers — air gap assessments require on-site presence, specialized tooling, and a methodology that doesn't touch production systems carelessly.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Jacksonville typically run $10,000–$150,000 depending on scope — a focused cybersecurity risk assessment for a single facility sits at the lower end, while a full control system modernization (architecture redesign, PLC reprogramming, network segmentation, and NERC CIP compliance documentation) lands at the top. Most consultants bill $175–$350/hour; fixed-fee engagements are more common for assessments, time-and-materials for implementation. Timelines range from two weeks for a targeted vulnerability assessment to six-plus months for a greenfield modernization.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is scoping an assessment when you actually need implementation support. An assessment tells you what's broken. If your team doesn't have the OT engineering bandwidth to execute the remediation roadmap, you're paying twice — once for the findings and once when you hire someone else to fix them. Scope the full engagement upfront.
