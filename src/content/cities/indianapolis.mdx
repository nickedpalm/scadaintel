## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Indianapolis

- **Verify certifications before the first call.** A GICSP from GIAC or a CAP from ISA is not the same as a general IT security cert. If someone leads with CISSP but can't name the ISA/IEC 62443 zones-and-conduits model, keep moving. Indianapolis has legitimate OT talent, but it also has IT consultants who've rebranded after reading one Dragos blog post.
- **Ask for vertical-specific references.** Indiana's industrial mix skews toward pharma, utilities, and discrete manufacturing. A consultant who's done NERC CIP gap assessments for an investor-owned utility (IPL / AES Indiana is right here) will approach a water/wastewater Purdue Model segmentation project very differently than someone who's only done oil and gas work in Texas.
- **Scope the engagement in writing before any SOW.** "SCADA assessment" means different things to different people — make sure you know whether you're buying a passive network discovery scan, a full architecture review, or hands-on remediation. Vague scopes produce vague deliverables and very concrete invoices.
- **Check their OT toolkit.** Legitimate consultants carry familiarity with Claroty, Dragos, or Nozomi for passive monitoring, and know their way around Rockwell, Siemens, and GE platforms — which dominate central Indiana's manufacturing floors. If they're proposing to run Nessus credentialed scans against your live PLCs, that's a red flag, not a methodology.
- **Confirm insurance and NDA terms upfront.** You're handing someone access to process control documentation. Errors & omissions coverage and a solid confidentiality agreement aren't optional extras — they're table stakes.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask the consultant to describe a time a client pushed back on a remediation recommendation and what happened. How they answer tells you whether they're advisors or just report-generators.

## What to Expect

Engagements in Indianapolis typically run $10,000 for a focused cybersecurity risk assessment on a single facility up to $150,000+ for a full architecture redesign, migration from legacy SCADA to a modern platform, or multi-site NERC CIP compliance program. Timeline varies from two weeks (assessment + report) to six months (design-through-commissioning). Most firms require a discovery phase before committing to a fixed-price SOW — that's normal.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake plant managers make is assuming the cheapest assessment protects them. A $12,000 vulnerability report that lists CVEs without prioritizing them against your actual process risk profile is worth roughly what you paid for it. The deliverable you want is a remediation roadmap ranked by operational impact, not a raw export from a scanning tool.
