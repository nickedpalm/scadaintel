## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Fort Worth

- **Match credentials to your system type.** A GICSP or ISA/IEC 62443 cert matters for cybersecurity and OT network work. A CAP or PE (Electrical/Controls) matters more for system architecture and PLC/HMI programming. Don't hire a cybersecurity specialist to redesign your SCADA architecture, and don't hire a controls engineer to do your NERC CIP compliance audit.
- **Ask specifically about your vendor stack.** Fort Worth-area utilities and industrial facilities run everything from legacy Allen-Bradley PLCs to modern Ignition SCADA platforms. "SCADA experience" means nothing if the consultant has never touched your historian or your HMI software.
- **Check for Texas-specific regulatory exposure.** If you're a water utility in Tarrant County or operating under TCEQ oversight, verify the consultant understands Texas's water system cybersecurity requirements, not just federal ones.
- **Demand a scoping call before any proposal.** Any consultant who quotes you a price before seeing your architecture diagrams and understanding your compliance posture is guessing. The range on these engagements ($10K–$150K) exists precisely because scope varies wildly.
- **Get references from similar facility types.** An oil & gas upstream SCADA project looks nothing like a municipal water treatment modernization. Ask for references from the same vertical you're in.

> **Pro Tip:** Fort Worth's industrial corridor along the Alliance corridor and Eagle Mountain–Saginaw area has significant manufacturing and logistics density. Consultants who've worked locally will understand the specific Oncor and Atmos Energy grid constraints that affect substation and metering SCADA integrations in this region.

## What to Expect

Engagements typically run $10,000–$150,000 depending on scope — a focused OT security assessment for a mid-size water plant sits in the $15K–$40K range, while a full SCADA modernization with PLC reprogramming, network segmentation, and documentation can push past $100K. Most consultants work in 4–12 week phases, with discovery and assessment front-loaded before any remediation or implementation work begins.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake operators make is hiring for the cheapest assessment, then being surprised when remediation isn't included. A $12,000 vulnerability report that hands you a list of 47 findings — with zero implementation support — can leave you worse off than before, because now you have a documented liability. Make sure your SOW specifies what deliverables you're actually getting.
