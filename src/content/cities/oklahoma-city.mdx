## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Oklahoma City

- **Verify credentials before the first call.** GICSP (GIAC's Global Industrial Cyber Security Professional) and CAP (ISA's Certified Automation Professional) aren't vanity letters — they represent actual hours inside control system environments. A PE license with an electrical or controls focus is a strong secondary signal for projects involving grid-tied infrastructure or regulated utilities.
- **Ask for Oklahoma-specific references.** The Oklahoma Corporation Commission has jurisdiction over a lot of the oil and gas infrastructure in this region, and navigating OCC compliance alongside EPA and NERC CIP requirements is a specialized skill. A consultant who's done it before can show you the paperwork.
- **Distinguish OT security from IT security.** Most cybersecurity firms in OKC can handle your firewalls. Very few understand ISA/IEC 62443 zone-and-conduit models, Purdue architecture, or why you can't just patch a Modicon PLC the way you'd patch a Windows server. If they lead with NIST 800-53 and never mention ISA-99, keep looking.
- **Scope the engagement before you talk price.** A SCADA assessment for a single water treatment plant and a full architecture redesign for a mid-continent pipeline system are not the same project. Get a written scope of work before any number gets floated — consultants who resist this are a red flag.
- **Look for familiarity with your specific platform.** Rockwell ControlLogix, Ignition SCADA, and GE iFIX are common in OKC's energy and municipal utility sector. A consultant who's never touched your HMI platform will spend your budget on their learning curve.

> **Pro Tip:** Oklahoma has a dense concentration of midstream and upstream oil and gas operators (think Oneok, Devon, and Continental's sphere of vendors and contractors). If you're in that space, ask specifically about NERC CIP compliance experience — even if you're not a bulk electric system operator today, M&A activity can change that fast, and consultants who already know the framework save you six months of catch-up.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Oklahoma City typically run $10,000 for a focused vulnerability assessment on a single site up to $150,000 or more for a full architecture redesign, network segmentation project, or multi-site OT security program. Most mid-range engagements — a risk assessment plus a remediation roadmap — land between $25,000 and $60,000 depending on site complexity and how much hands-on implementation support you need. Timeline is usually 4–12 weeks for an assessment phase, longer if you're pulling in implementation.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is scoping an "assessment" when you actually need an "implementation." Assessments produce reports. Implementations produce working systems. If your goal is to actually fix the vulnerabilities, budget for both — and get line-item clarity on which deliverables are included before you sign anything.
