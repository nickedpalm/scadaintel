## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Louisville

- **Verify credentials before the first call.** GICSP (from GIAC) and the ISA/IEC 62443 Cybersecurity Certificate are the two that actually mean something for OT security engagements. CAP matters for automation design work. A PE license (Electrical or Control Systems) is worth asking about if your project touches utility interconnects or gets pulled into regulatory review.

- **Ask specifically about Kentucky's utility landscape.** LG&E and Kentucky Utilities operate under NERC CIP compliance requirements — if your facility has any exposure to bulk electric systems, you want a consultant who has done NERC CIP gap assessments before, not one who's read the standards. This is a real differentiator that separates generalists from specialists.

- **Check their protocol stack experience against your actual equipment.** A consultant who lives in Modbus/DNP3 isn't automatically the right call for a Rockwell/Allen-Bradley-heavy plant running EtherNet/IP. Get specific about your PLCs, HMIs, and historian software before you assume a resume full of "industrial cybersecurity" covers your stack.

- **Understand what you're actually buying.** Some engagements are pure advisory — architecture review, vulnerability assessment, a roadmap document. Others include hands-on implementation (network segmentation, firewall rule changes, PLC code modifications). Know which you need and make sure the contract spells it out. Scope creep on OT projects is expensive and disruptive.

- **Ask for references from similar industries.** Louisville's industrial base spans automotive, food & beverage, bourbon distilleries, water/wastewater, and logistics — a consultant with deep utility experience may have thin manufacturing credentials, or vice versa. A reference from a plant similar to yours is worth more than a generic case study.

> **Pro Tip:** Louisville Metro Sewer District and Louisville Water Company have both undergone significant OT modernization projects in recent years. If a consultant can't name a single water/wastewater project in the region or discuss what those engagements typically look like, that's a gap worth probing.

## What to Expect

SCADA consulting engagements in Louisville typically run $10,000 on the low end for a scoped vulnerability assessment or architecture review, and $150,000 or more for full system modernization projects with multi-site scope and implementation support — expect the higher end if your project spans multiple facilities or requires NERC CIP documentation deliverables. Most engagements kick off with a 2–4 week discovery and assessment phase before any remediation work begins. Timeline from first call to delivered report is usually 6–10 weeks for assessment-only work; implementation projects scale from there.

> **Reality Check:** The single biggest pricing mistake is scoping an engagement as "assessment only" when what you actually need includes remediation. Consultants who quote low on the front end and then bill heavily for implementation changes aren't being dishonest — you just told them to quote a different project. Be specific about deliverables, not just phases.
