## How to Choose a SCADA Consultant in Philadelphia

- **Verify OT-specific credentials, not just cybersecurity certs.** A CISM is a solid baseline, but for Philadelphia-area engagements — especially PECO/Exelon-adjacent grid work or PWSA water treatment — you want someone with a GICSP or ISA/IEC 62443 certificate. General IT security consultants routinely underbid OT projects and underestimate the air-gap complexity.
- **Ask for Pennsylvania-specific compliance experience.** Pennsylvania DEP has its own water facility reporting requirements layered on top of EPA AWIA 2018 mandates. A consultant who's worked with Philadelphia Water Department or similar PA utilities already speaks the regulatory dialect — that alone can cut weeks off a compliance review.
- **Check their PLC/HMI vendor depth.** There's a meaningful difference between a consultant who's programmed Allen-Bradley ControlLogix in the field versus one who's only reviewed architecture diagrams. Ask for the specific platforms they've deployed against. The Philly industrial corridor — Girard Point, Navy Yard, Northeast industrial parks — runs a wide mix of Siemens, Rockwell, and legacy Modicon systems.
- **Require a scoped deliverable list upfront.** Good SCADA consultants produce architecture diagrams, network segmentation plans, vulnerability assessments, and remediation roadmaps. If a firm can't enumerate their deliverables in the first conversation, they're billing by the hour hoping for scope creep.
- **Confirm NERC CIP familiarity if you're utility-adjacent.** PJM Interconnection coordinates a massive slice of the regional grid, and any consultant touching transmission-related SCADA at a Pennsylvania utility needs to know CIP-002 through CIP-013 cold.

> **Pro Tip:** Pennsylvania requires PE licensure for certain control systems work on public infrastructure projects. If your engagement involves municipal water, wastewater, or public power infrastructure, confirm your consultant holds — or is working under — a licensed PE. It's not just a credential flex; it's a liability shield.

## What to Expect

Philadelphia-area SCADA engagements typically run $10,000–$150,000 depending on scope: a focused OT cybersecurity risk assessment for a single facility lands at the lower end, while a full control system modernization — new HMI architecture, PLC reprogramming, network segmentation, and a NERC CIP compliance review — can push into six figures. Project timelines range from 3 weeks for a scoped audit to 6+ months for greenfield implementations. Most experienced consultants front-load a discovery phase before quoting, which is the right call — any firm giving you a fixed price before seeing your P&IDs is guessing.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is scoping an "assessment" when you actually need an "implementation." Assessments find the problems. Implementations fix them. Many buyers get a $15,000 vulnerability report, discover they need $80,000 of remediation work, and feel surprised — because no one was direct about what the deliverable actually was. Push for clarity on what happens *after* the report.
