## Why New York Is Harder Than It Looks

Here's what most people miss: New York's size works against you when you're hiring for OT/SCADA work.

The city has a massive, well-documented IT consulting scene. Clutch and DesignRush list hundreds of firms. The problem is that SCADA sits in operational technology — a completely different discipline from the cloud, SaaS, and enterprise software work that dominates those lists. You can spend a week on Clutch filtering for automation firms and come back with zero results specifically tagged for Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition.

The firms that actually do this work — PLC/HMI programming, network segmentation for OT environments, NERC CIP compliance reviews — are often quieter, smaller, and regionally positioned rather than headquartered in midtown.

> **Reality Check:** A general IT consulting firm is not a SCADA integrator. The discipline gap is real — OT cybersecurity, ladder logic programming, and historian configuration are not skills that transfer from enterprise software work. Hiring the wrong type of firm is how you end up with a network architecture that looks clean on paper and fails the moment a field device goes offline.

## What to Require Before You Sign Anything

The credentialing landscape for SCADA work isn't as standardized as, say, electrical licensing — which makes vetting harder and more important.

**Minimum credentials worth requiring:**
- **ISO-9001** — quality management certification; Lashley Cohen holds it, and it's a reasonable baseline to filter by
- **GICSP** (Global Industrial Cyber Security Professional) or **CAP** (Certified Automation Professional) — shows the engineer has formally trained on OT security, not just IT security
- **ISA/IEC 62443** familiarity — the dominant standard for industrial cybersecurity; any serious OT firm should speak this language fluently
- **CMMI Level 3** — relevant for larger-scope engagements where repeatable process matters

> **Pro Tip:** Ask candidates to walk you through their approach to network segmentation between OT and enterprise IT. A strong answer mentions firewalls (Palo Alto or Juniper), VPN architecture, and the specific risks of bridging your SCADA network to corporate wi-fi. A weak answer talks about software licenses. That single question will filter out 60% of firms that shouldn't be in the room.

## What You'll Pay

New York pricing for SCADA consulting follows the broader IT consulting market upward, but with less competition at the specialized end.

General regional rates from Clutch/DesignRush data: **$100–250/hr** for project work. Full project engagements run **$10K to $500K+** depending on scope — a cybersecurity risk assessment for a mid-sized water system looks very different from a full SCADA platform migration.

Freelance SCADA specialists on Upwork run **$50–150/hr** and can handle narrower scopes: configuration review, PLC programming support, remote architecture consultation. For smaller industrial operators who don't need a full systems integrator, this is a legitimate option that most people overlook.

Nobody tells you this: the boutique regional firms rarely post rates publicly. Budget 2–3 weeks to get comparable proposals if you're running a competitive process.
