## How to Evaluate a SCADA Consultant (The Framework That Actually Works)

The hiring mistake I see most often: evaluating SCADA consultants like you'd evaluate an IT vendor. You're not buying a help desk contract. You're buying someone to touch systems that control physical infrastructure — pumps, valves, turbines. The consequences of a bad hire aren't a slow laptop; they're operational downtime or worse.

Here's the comparison that matters:

| What IT Consultants Offer | What OT/SCADA Specialists Offer |
|---|---|
| Network monitoring, endpoint security | OT network segmentation, DMZ design |
| Cloud migration, SaaS integration | Historian configuration, data diode implementation |
| General cybersecurity frameworks | ICS-specific frameworks (ISA/IEC 62443, NERC CIP) |
| Help desk, patch management | PLC/HMI programming, field commissioning |
| Vendor-agnostic IT credentials | GICSP, CAP, ISA certifications |

The firms worth talking to in Miami will lead with OT experience, not IT experience. Their project references will include utilities, municipalities, manufacturing plants — not law firms or healthcare networks.

## What the Engagement Actually Looks Like

Nobody tells you this part: a SCADA consulting engagement isn't one thing. It's a spectrum depending on where you are in your project.

Most engagements in Miami's water/wastewater and utility sector start with one of three triggers: a control system approaching end-of-life (the 20-year-old Wonderware installation that nobody knows how to support), a cybersecurity incident or near-miss, or a regulatory audit that surfaced gaps.

Deliverables vary accordingly:

- **Architecture assessment:** Current-state documentation, gap analysis, remediation roadmap
- **Cybersecurity risk assessment:** OT vulnerability scan, network segmentation review, NERC CIP or ICS-CERT framework evaluation
- **Implementation support:** PLC/HMI reprogramming, historian migration, field commissioning
- **Ongoing advisory:** Periodic audits, patch management strategy, incident response planning

The architecture assessment is usually where to start if you're not sure what you need. It gives you a defensible baseline — useful internally, useful with regulators, useful when scoping a larger modernization project.
