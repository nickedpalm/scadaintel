## Why Chicago's SCADA Market Is Different

Chicago isn't a generic tech hub. It's a manufacturing and logistics backbone — water treatment, food processing, rail infrastructure, industrial automation. That means the SCADA consultants operating here have context that generalist IT shops simply don't.

The job market confirms it. [ZipRecruiter data from April 2026](https://www.ziprecruiter.com) puts the average SCADA consultant rate in Chicago at **$42.81/hour**, with a range spanning **$30 to $89**. Glassdoor shows **72 open SCADA engineer roles** in the metro; Indeed lists **142**. When a specialized field has that many openings, it tells you two things: demand is real, and supply is constrained.

Constrained supply means you're competing with local plants and utilities for the same pool of talent. If you need a consultant for a modernization project, don't assume you can find someone in two weeks.

## What Chicago SCADA Consultants Actually Do

Here's what most people miss: "SCADA consultant" covers a wide range of work, and not every firm does all of it.

The core engagement types look like this:

| Engagement Type | What's Involved | Typical Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Legacy system modernization | Upgrade aging PLCs, HMIs, historian software | Equipment end-of-life, vendor support ending |
| Turnkey SCADA deployment | Architecture, programming, commissioning | New facility or major expansion |
| OT cybersecurity audit | Network segmentation review, vulnerability assessment, NERC CIP | Post-incident, compliance requirement |
| HMI/SCADA programming | Configuration, integration with manufacturing systems | New line, process change |
| Ongoing support/retainer | Monitoring, troubleshooting, change management | Operational continuity |

The cybersecurity piece deserves special attention. The **2nd ICS/SCADA Cybersecurity Symposium** is scheduled in Chicago for **June 16–18, 2026**, focused specifically on threat intelligence and critical infrastructure protection. That's not a coincidence — it's a response to real incident trends in the ICS space. If your facility hasn't had an OT security review in the last 18 months, that's a gap worth closing before someone closes it for you.

> **Reality Check:** A consultant who's great at HMI programming may have zero experience with OT network segmentation. These are different skill sets. Don't assume SCADA expertise in one area transfers automatically to another — ask directly about their experience with your specific use case.

## Evaluating Chicago Consultants: What Actually Matters

The résumé-level credentials to look for: **GICSP** (Global Industrial Cyber Security Professional), **CAP** (Certified Automation Professional from ISA), and familiarity with **ISA/IEC 62443** standards. These aren't just alphabet soup — they signal someone who's engaged with industrial security as a discipline, not just as a firewall configuration task.

Two firms with Chicago presence worth knowing:

**DMC, Inc.** runs a large engineering team with a global SCADA/HMI practice. They handle the full stack — architecture through commissioning — and have documented experience in manufacturing automation and facility modernization. Good fit for complex, multi-site projects.

**Empowered Automation Solutions LLC** is Chicago-based and specializes in HMI/SCADA implementation. They offer demos for local projects, which is a low-friction way to evaluate fit before committing to a scope.

Neither is a magic bullet. Your facility's context — whether you're running Ignition, Wonderware, FactoryTalk, or something proprietary — matters a lot when evaluating who's actually equipped to help.

> **Pro Tip:** Before your first call with any consultant, pull together your current architecture diagram (even a rough one), your historian and HMI software versions, and a list of your top 3 pain points. Consultants who ask good clarifying questions in the first 30 minutes are worth more than ones who immediately start pitching solutions.

## Practical Bottom Line

If you're a plant engineer or operations manager evaluating consultants right now, here's the short checklist:

1. **Define your scope first.** Modernization, cybersecurity audit, HMI programming, and turnkey deployment are different engagements with different skill requirements.
2. **Budget $42–$89/hour for quality work.** The $30 floor exists, but don't anchor there for anything involving critical infrastructure.
3. **Ask for credentials.** GICSP or CAP aren't mandatory, but their absence should prompt harder questions about hands-on OT experience.
4. **Start early.** With 72–142 open roles competing for the same talent, experienced consultants have options. If you need someone by Q3, start conversations now.
5. **Check their incident response posture.** In 2026, a SCADA consultant who can't speak intelligently about ICS cybersecurity is leaving you exposed.

The Chicago market has the talent. The work is finding the right match for your specific system, your timeline, and your risk tolerance.
